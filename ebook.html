FAQ

What does the La Trobe eBureau offer for La Trobe academic authors?

The eBureau provides academics with professional editing, design, copyright and online publishing services. We coordinate manuscript editing services to address the editorial needs of authors and ensure high quality publications.The eBureau applies a distinctive identity design to all publications and coordinates additional external design services as required.

To ensure all published third party materials in eBureau textbooks meet statutory requirements the eBureau provides copyright status checking relating to the use and reproduction of images, audio, video, diagrams and text. Etextbooks published by the eBureau are available in a number of formats to ensure equity of access for La Trobe students.

Promoting scholarship of LTU academics?

The eBureau promotes the scholarship of LTU academics. It ensures that their works are highly visible, and discoverable. All of the published etextbooks are available to La Trobe students via Reading Lists or the LMS, and to both students and to the wider community via either the library catalogue, the La Trobe eBureau website, or Google.

How does the La Trobe eBureau support the University Digital Learning Strategy?

The La Trobe eBureau directly supports blended or online modes of learning and supports the University’s Digital Learning Strategy aspiration for “accessibility and flexibility” and for learning resources to be ‘everywhere’.

How will my students benefit from using La Trobe eBureau?

La Trobe eBureau textbooks are high quality, tailored education resources, written by La Trobe academics specifically to support La Trobe courses and subjects.

eBureau etextbooks are openly available free of charge from any location, on any device, at any time, including any interactive multi-media elements that may have been included. Etextbooks published by the eBureau are available in a number of formats to ensure equality of access for La Trobe students.
